What distunguishes Shakespeare from other poets? Many times have individuals claimed his writing as the pinnacle of the written, and acted, word.

So why then do so many schoolboys groan at the thought of having to sit through any of his plays? I believe that there is much truth to this, but also that there is something more, something less obvious, yet more significant. If is the age of the language itself, then poets contemoprary to his time ought to be just as difficult to read and around as alien to a less educated person's modern sensibilities as his was.
